Data First

Every thing starts from Data
From understanding the end goal and the problems
...to knowing your target audience
...to determining what matters to the readers

If we have data, let’s look at data. If all we have are opinions, let’s go with mine.

Jim Barksdale, former CEO of Netscape
One of my favorite quotes recently is this.
Because really, we all have different opinions and we all honestly believe we are correct. So data is the only thing that can resolve these differences.

Data prevents us from getting distracted by less important things

Like I said earlier, data makes us determine what matters most to the readers Product owners may want to have the latest features they see on another website but are they really needed. Will the results be worth the effort.

Data forces us to focus on the important things

Data allows us to zero in on the most pressing matters. We may not need that thumbnail there, or we may not need so many stories displayed on a homepage. Reduce cognitive overload for a better UX

Style Guide

A Style Guide is a set of standards for the writing and design of documents
A style guide establishes and enforces style to improve communication.
Brad Frost categorized style guides into a few flavours
  • Brand Identity
  • Design Language
  • Voice and Tone
  • Writing
  • Patterns
  • Code
http://bradfrost.com/blog/post/style-guides/
Brad Frost is one of the most popular UX specialist and he categorizes Style Guides into 6 different flavors.
For us in DDTech, these 3 are crucial.

Prescribes Typography, Color Palettes, Page Components and other Patterns

How many fonts do we need? What colors do we use? How does this component work if the story does not come with an image? How much space shoul this block take up on tablet?

Creates a Shared Vocabulary

Often times we call things differently and this can lead to a lot of confusion
A common vocabulary ...

Eliminates Waste of Effort

Us developers, are all very familiar with the problem of being presented with a mock-up done in photoshop with content all aligned perfectly and components that we have no idea where we are going to get them from. Worst is if this design has already been approved and when we ask the designers, they too are clueless

Promotes UI Consistency

A page component is defined by a set of rules (padding, color, typeface, etc)
